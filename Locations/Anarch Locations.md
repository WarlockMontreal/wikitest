# Anarch Territory: Montreal Locations

## St Helens Island
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **La Ronde:** A large amusement park with scenic views of the Montreal skyline and St. Lawrence River. It is a major destination for tourists and locals during warmer months.
* **Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve:** A renowned Formula 1 race track used for recreational cycling and walking outside of race events.
* **Stewart Museum:** Located in a historic military building on the western tip of the island, focusing on colonial history and New France.
* **Parc Jean-Drapeau:** A massive green space hosting festivals, concerts, and cultural events throughout the year.
* **The Asylum:** A clandestine haven and "hidden gem" of the island, serving as a playground for both mortals and Kindred.

## Saint Leonard
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Leonardo Da Vinci Centre:** A cultural complex hosting community gatherings and art; it remains calm and dimly lit at night.
* **Wilfrid-Bastien Park:** A residential green space featuring a dense canopy and a small pond, largely empty after dusk.
* **Disused Tunnel (Boulevard Lacordaire):** A long-sealed pedestrian tunnel beneath the boulevard, marked by graffiti and echoing silence.
* **Saint-Léonard Cavern Entrance:** A fenced-off geological site near a modest park with rumored deep, unexplored passages.

## Montreal East
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Hôpital Vétérinaire de Montréal-Est:** A utilitarian brick building between industrial lots.
    * **Bagger DC:** 2
* **Refinery Overlook:** A hill overlooking industrial pipes and chimneys that glow orange with safety flares at night.
* **Rue Notre-Dame Industrial Strip:** A stretch of warehouses and service yards that is mostly deserted after sunset.
* **Waterfront Rail Yard:** Parallel to the St. Lawrence River, this yard smells of rust and diesel and is covered in graffiti.

## Riviere des Prairies
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Bois-de-l'Anjou Nature Park:** A dense woodland area with trails winding through pine and birch groves.
    * **Farmer DC:** 3
* **CLSC Rivière-des-Prairies:** A utilitarian public health facility with long, dated corridors.
* **Rivière-des-Prairies Train Station:** A quiet commuter station with a subdued atmosphere and minimal signage.
* **Église Saint-Joseph-de-Rivière-des-Prairies:** A classic Catholic stone church featuring a single bell tower and a sense of stillness.

## The Gay Village
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Rue Sainte-Catherine Est:** A pedestrian-friendly stretch of bars and murals that shifts to a neon glow at night.
* **Beaudry Metro Station:** A gateway to the neighborhood known for its colorful columns and echoing tiled corridors.
* **Parc de l’Espoir:** A small, triangular park with a reflective, quiet mood.
* **The Loft Building:** An old industrial structure converted into lofts, often filled with music or strange conversations.
* **The Heart:** An eccentric BDSM club owned by Sasha; a place where the Ministry hides in plain sight under red lights.
