# Contested Territory: Montreal Locations

## The Underground City
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Complexe Desjardins**: A vast, multi-level indoor plaza with a massive central atrium that fills with a dim glow by night. Wide corridors connect various passageways where footsteps echo endlessly in the quiet hours.
* **Central Station Tunnels**: Lined with dimly lit tiles, these tunnels connect the metro to office complexes. Pedestrians move quietly through a maze of passages where the sounds of the city above are barely perceptible.
* **La Cité Complex**: An extensive network of underground shops and residential spaces featuring dark, labyrinthine hallways and isolated pathways that rarely see traversal.
* **St. James the Apostle Chapel**: A small, historic chapel with low vaulted ceilings and stone walls nestled in a secluded section of the Underground City. Stained-glass windows cast faint colors onto worn wooden pews in a tangible atmosphere of silence.
* **Square-Victoria Passages**: A maze of winding corridors near the business district with scattered seating areas. The atmosphere is a blend of modern design and dimly lit spaces, broken only by distant echoes and the hum of air conditioning.

## The Old Port
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Place Jacques-Cartier**: A historic square lined with cobblestone paths and stone façades under old-fashioned streetlights.
    * **Extortionist DC**: 4
* **Dorchester Square**: A manicured urban park featuring wide gravel pathways and a grand monument with a bronzed lion. At night, streetlamps cast long shadows over the pathways in the heart of downtown.
* **Nacarat Bar**: A sophisticated rooftop oasis atop the Queen Elizabeth hotel featuring live jazz and plush crimson decor. It offers panoramic views of the skyline and the St. Lawrence River.
* **The Labyrinth of Old Montreal’s Alleys**: Narrow, dimly lit paths that twist between old stone buildings, often ending in secluded, shadowed courtyards.

## Mont Royal Plateau
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Rue Saint-Denis**: A lively avenue known for historic facades, boutiques, and a dynamic social scene that transforms into a bustling nightlife.
* **Mount Royal Cemetery**: A sprawling cemetery on the northern slope of the mountain with ancient trees and historic monuments.
    * **Graverobber DC**: 2
* **Carré Saint-Louis**: A picturesque park surrounded by Victorian-style row houses and art galleries, capturing a bohemian spirit.
* **Mount Royal Summit and Cross**: The peak of the mountain featuring a tall illuminated cross and panoramic views of the city.
    * **Roadside Killer (Tourists) DC**: 3

## Little Italy
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Church of the Madonna della Difesa**: A dominating brick church with vibrant stained-glass and a crypt holding the remains of past generations.
* **Nestor's Bar**: A hidden, dimly lit speakeasy furnished with plush velvet and dark wood.
* **Little Italy Market (Jean-Talon Market)**: A sprawling market with stone floors and high ceilings that connect deeply to the local community.
* **Evans Funeral House**: A modern, minimalist funeral home with a peaceful atmosphere and viewing rooms designed for privacy.
    * **Portillon (Hecata leadership)**: ●●●●
* **Rialto Theatre**: An elegant art deco building with marble floors, a massive chandelier, and an atmosphere of old-world grandeur.

## Ahuntsic Cartierville
**General Hunting DC:** 6

* **Fleury Hospital**: A mid-20th-century hospital with narrow, eerie hallways that echo with the hum of machinery at night.
    * **Bagger DC**: 3
    * **Grim Reaper DC**: 2
* **Saint-Benoît Church**: A historical church that is largely empty at night; Kindred sensitive to holy symbols may experience discomfort here.
* **Ahuntsic-Cartierville Public Library**: A squat, unassuming brick and stone building partially obscured by overgrown ivy.
* **Parc Ahuntsic**: A peaceful urban retreat with winding paths and mature trees. The central lake reflects the moonlight, providing an eerie calm.
